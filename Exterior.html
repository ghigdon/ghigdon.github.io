The Key Fob

The Chevy Volt's key fob serves many functions. Although the keyfob has a switchblade-style key, the switchblade is not actually used. Instead, the fob acts as a proximity key, which means that it emits radio waves that informs the computer system how far it is from any part of the car at any time. This technology allows you to perform functions such as keyless entry and push-button start. Of course, the buttons on the key fob also perform their own functions.

Key Fob Button Function
Unlocks Vehicle Doors and trunk; Disarms alarm system.
Locks Vehicle Doors and trunk; Arms alarm system.
Opens the charger port.
Starts and conditions the vehicle remotely so that the climate control system can warm or cool the cabin.
Sets off the car's alarm system (lights and horn) OR Stops the car's alarm system.

When the key fob comes within a short range of the vehicle, the keyless entry buttons may be pressed to lock or unlock the cabin's doors.

All four tires come equipped with tire pressure monitors, which enable the car to alert the driver when their tire pressure in a specific wheel gets to unsafe levels.
